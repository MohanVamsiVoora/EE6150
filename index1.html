Little's thereom:

The long-term average number L of customers in a stationary system is equal to the long-term average effective  arrival rate λ multiplied by the average time W that a customer spends in the system.
$$L = \lambda W$$ 
Although it looks intuitively easy, it is quite a remarkable result, as the relationship is not influenced by    the arrival process distribution, the service distribution, the service order, or practically anything else.

We can thus consider any sub-part of the system as our new system and apply Little's theorem. Also as it is not dependant on distribution, try reducing the problem to trivial distributions to apply Little's theorem (Not much of use)
